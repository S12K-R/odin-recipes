French Toast

Description

The french toast, is a bread recipe that works with many types of bread: white, whole wheat, brioche, cinnamon-raisin, Italian, or French. It is known to be a sweet and delicious option to make with bread for breakfast and it's quite easy to make as well, you'll only need a few ingredients, a skillet, and a good recipe which will be provided in the next section.

Ingredients (for 3 servings)

  • 6 thick white bread slices
  • 2 large eggs
  • ⅔ milk cups
  • 1 vanilla extract teaspoon and ¼ ground cinnamon teaspoon (Optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 unsalted butter teaspoon (or more if required)

Steps

  1. Whisk milk, eggs, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt together in a shallow bowl.
  2. Lightly butter a griddle and heat over medium-high heat.
  3. Dunk bread in the egg mixture, soaking both sides. Transfer to the hot skillet and cook until golden, 3 to 4 minutes per side. Serve hot.
Is french toast actually French?

French toast (pain perdu in French) isn't actually from France. Legend has it that a man named Joseph French from the U.S. created the sweet breakfast dish in 1724 and named it after himself. However, that's probably not true: People were frying milk- and egg-soaked bread in Ancient Rome, according to early recipes.

It's more likely that modern French toast was invented by 15th-century cooks who were on a mission to use up stale bread.
